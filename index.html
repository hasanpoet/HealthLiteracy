Common Healthy Eating Myths 🤔

Tap a card to reveal the truth.

Myth: Eating too much of one food is bad for you ▾
Truth: Health comes from balance: sleep 😴, movement 🏃‍♀️, variety 🍎, and self‑care ❤️.
Myth: Eating healthy is too expensive ▾
Truth: It doesn’t have to be. Beans and lentils are affordable, nutritious, and filling.
Myth: Canned/frozen fruits & veggies are less healthy ▾
Truth: They can be just as nutritious. Look for “no salt added” or “packed in 100% juice”.
Myth: You must give up favourite foods ▾
Truth: Not true! 🎉 Enjoy treats in balance and variety.

Common Exercise Myths 🤔

Tap a card to reveal the truth.

Myth Exercise has to be hard to help. ▾
Truth: Any movement counts. Start small and build up. Exercise should feel good—not painful. It’s okay to stop or try something new if it’s too difficult. Enjoyable movement builds a habit that lasts.
Myth You should avoid rest. ▾
Truth: Rest helps your body and mind recharge—especially after lots of movement. Recovery supports muscle repair and keeps you feeling your best. A chill day is part of staying healthy, too!

About the Team

This project started as part of a community-engaged learning course that the core members of our team, Subah, Helena, and Sweta, are taking as part of their master of Health Information Science degree at the University of Western Ontario in collaboration with the Ontario Centre for Talent Development. The primary objective is to create a tailored, educational webpage to enhance primary and secondary students’ understanding of essential health topics and promote healthy living habits. Ideally, we hope to expand by developing an app to make the platform more accessible and appealing to our target audience. As our team grows and our project further develops, we plan to incorporate more interactive components into our prototype to create a more engaging and impactful product. Currently, we have developped modules for healthy eating and exercise, but we are wishing to expand our content and create additional modules for topics such as growing bodies, sexual health, and mental well-being. This initiative is ongoing, with our team continuously reviewing and refining content and delivery methods to develop the best possible prototype for primary and secondary students!
